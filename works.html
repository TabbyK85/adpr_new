A line or two.

Sometimes I write unserious stories
about serious people. The broken ones.
A sort of character kintsugi.

You can find out how they end on my Patreon.
Just as soon as I get one.

Atitlan | 2020

Non deciduous

I’m reading lots of letters from the women I admire. I have a rucksack full of newspapers and handwritten notes like a Twainian protagonist wrapped in a nightmare wrapped in an Imperial Wizard’s purple robe. They’re telling little girls to be strong and wild and free. I don’t believe in anything anymore but the push and pull of the water, my body against it, and what it is to almost drown.

Marfa | 2019

Misrememberings

That breath was the worst of it, because it made it all mine, all the pain. The sigh is a vessel.

Boulder | 2019

T.C.

Loving is an avalanche and I am being buried. I wear a knit cap and pretend to be a stick-up kid, tug it right while I pour orange juice into a plastic cup, then vodka. But mostly vodka. In between smoking rolled cigarettes and pulling loose tobacco from the tip of my tongue, I think about how terrible it would be to live here, to be her, forever.

New Orleans | 2019

The End of the World

Takeaways: schadenfreude, curio cabinets, the exes who—for 90 days—walked toward and not away from one another, relationships as Russian nesting dolls, my second Icarus reference in as many days.

Gruene | 2019

Ticky Tacky

Where I grew up used to fit inside of a postcard, with checkered valances and stick-figure drawings in which I’m twice the size of my father and the dog fits in my front pocket. And now where I came from is a hole in the ground and maybe my mom’s things are in 1994.

Cannon Beach | 2018

Duplex

It has been raining and my jacket smells damp and like every meal I’ve ever eaten. I am looking for you with all the lights off; it is only just dusk. You don’t reach for me; I take my toothbrush and a change of clothes.
